For some reason, I don't remember why, the other day I logged into my LinkedIn account. Probably, because I saw some clickbait email message. In any case as I briefly scanned the stream of posts spewing from the homepage, one caught my eye. The post immediately set off alarm bells in my pre-frontal lobe for its egregiousness. Before I explain why, here's a screenshot so you can form your own first impression.

![Tim Cook retirement]({{ site.baseurl }}/assets/images/posts/grading-by-greed/tim_cook_retirement.png)

I'm not sure who this guy James Hawkins is, but he appears to be some kind of venture capitalist. Not sure what his claim to fame is, but apparently he has seen fit to give Tim Cook a performance review. Maybe he's not serious? Because when I walk through the critique it immediately falls apart in a big way. At the same time it exposes what's wrong with, for lack of a better bucket, the Epstein Class (AKA The Man, Fat Cats, Billionaires, 100 millionaires, Revolving Door C-teamers). These guys (and gals) drone predictably on about innovation, value creation and nirvanic corporate culture creation but are really mainly driven by greed and gratuitous padding of their resumes. Full disclosure: I spent over a decade serving The Man.

So when Tim Cook took over as Apple CEO he should have liquidated Apple and thrown all the money into Nvidia. I really think James is joking. Then Tim would just sit around reading novels for 15 years. Even better, Apple would have zero expenses, zero employees and zero suppliers. Oh wait, but maybe Apple actually built a few useful pieces of hardware under Tim that many people today might consider indispensable. Maybe I'm perilously close to Apple fanboy status but I do find this Macbook I'm writing on, my iPhone, Apple watch, Airpods, Apple Music, Apple TV and my daughter's iPad to be pretty useful tools. Not to mention the Apple stock in my IRA. 

Whether or not we should be chained to electronic devices and the Internet is the topic for another blog post.

But you know, James really has a point. And I think we could extend his criticism to anyone who had spare cash or could get their hands on cash when Cook took over as CEO at Apple. They all should have invested in Nvidia. Boy, we are all looking pretty stupid now. But wait a second. Isn't that called a Pyramid Scheme?
