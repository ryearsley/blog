I have a subscription to Apple News but these days I'm spending less time there, or at least trying to. I like to follow geopolitics both from an economic and political perspective. In the end, everything inevitably boils down to economics. There are some excellent podcasts I listen to that regularly present nuanced, fact based analyses with more objectivity and detail than anything you might find on the Apple News stream. 

Recently the FTC chairman sent a letter to Apple claiming that Apple editors consistently exhibit a [bias][apple_news_bias] toward "liberal" leaning news sources. Interesting. In my experience the Apple News stream is at best innocuous but generally populated with vapid "news stories" masquerading as journalism. And those are the "Top Stories". As you go down further into the feed the news part quickly evaporates into lighter topics like the best electric car to buy and Buzz Feed top ten lists (at least on my feed).

It's really surprising to me then that President Trump now has a beef with Apple News. Then again, what doesn't he have a beef with other than his own greatness? 

But back to today. Let's take a look at today's stream. I assume that in theory the stories are sorted in order of import descending since we're talking about "Top Stories". Coming in at number three from the Washington Post:

> King Charles's rare state visit offers U.K. a chance to mend ties with Trump.

![King Charles visits]({{ site.baseurl }}/assets/images/posts/the-king-comes-to-america/king_charles_visits.png)

Number three today? Really? I care about kings and their diplomatic wherewithal? Americans care about kings? Isn't there a No Kings movement that has been brought back to life? Didn't we get rid of the British 250 years ago because they had a king telling us what to do? And why do the British still have a king anyway? A guy who inherits a bunch of land, money, castles and some sort of god given credibility just by being born. The only place kings (and princesses) possibly ought to exist is in a Disney movie. What's the fascination? And why would Britain want to mend anything with Trump after being treated as a second class vassal state? I won't be clicking on that link. Merely an instructive headline that checks the vapid box.

[apple_news_bias]: https://www.washingtontimes.com/news/2026/mar/17/trump-puts-apple-notice-news-bias/

Let's take a look at another headline, coming in at number five.

> "Call us," Trump tells Iran as Putin hosts Tehran's top diplomat with peace talks stalled.

![Iran goes to Russia]({{ site.baseurl }}/assets/images/posts/the-king-comes-to-america/iran_goes_to_russia.png)

Let's unpack this headline. If you've been following the U.S. / Iran diplomatic efforts you'll know that Iran sent its foreign minister to Pakistan to meet with the Pakistanis alone. Iran had no intention of meeting with anyone from the U.S. At first Trump was excited to hear about Iran's visit to Pakistan. 

> Hey, I heard you're going to be in Pakistan again? Maybe we can meet up for coffee?

Trump rallied Jared and Steve to rush back to Pakistan. But Iran made clear that they wouldn't be meeting with any U.S. delegation. So Trump cancelled Jared and Steve's flight. Now Trump is asking Iran to give him a call. It all sounds like jilted boyfriend syndrome to me. Maybe Iran has finally had enough of the toxic relationship. You can't blame them for not wanting to negotiate a new global balance of power with two real estate developers. It's a real embarrassment. Iran probably could benefit from a restraining order on the U.S., considering every time Iran is at the table with the United States a sneak attack happens.
